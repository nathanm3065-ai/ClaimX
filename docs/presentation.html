Slide 1

Context and Why Now

Challenges and Impact

  • Business problem: traditional claims handling is slow, labor-intensive, and inconsistent.
  • Challenges: manual FNOL intake, triage, and adjudication create long cycle times; inconsistent decisions drive claims leakage and missed fraud.
  • Impact: customers wait days/weeks → churn; adjusters stuck on routine claims; leakage & fraud erode the loss ratio.

Strategic Imperative — why now

  • Rising claim volumes and severity inflation strain capacity.
  • Customers expect instant, digital-first resolution.
  • Carriers must scale lean and modernize core claims operations.

Shift in Operating Model

  • AI moves from back-office analytics to the front line of the claims workflow.
  • It reads submissions, assesses damage, scores fraud, and recommends decisions in seconds.
  • Humans shift to handling exceptions and making the final call.

Slide 2

Current Challenges and Gaps

Existing process (manual)

  • Customer calls/emails FNOL → rep keys in data.
  • Claim sits in a queue for manual triage.
  • Adjuster reads notes, requests photos, estimates damage by hand.
  • Fraud caught only if an adjuster happens to notice.
  • Decisions & settlements vary by individual.

Pain points & gaps

  • Manual activities: re-keying data, reading free text, chasing missing info.
  • Productivity: trivial and complex claims share one queue.
  • Inconsistency: no standard severity / fraud / settlement logic.
  • Fraud control: reactive and person-dependent.
  • Technology gap: systems of record, not systems of judgment.
  • Business limitation: can't scale volume without headcount.

Vision and Future State

What ClaimX enables

  • ⚡ Faster outcomes — instant AI triage at submission.
  • 🎯 Consistent decisions — every claim scored the same way.
  • 😀 Better experience — real-time status, quick resolution.
  • 💸 Less effort & leakage — evidence-based settlement.

Strategic outcomes

  • Lower cost-per-claim and reduced cycle time.
  • Improved loss ratio via consistency + fraud control.
  • Adjuster capacity redirected to complex, high-value claims.

Future-state operating model

  • People: adjusters become exception-handlers and decision-makers.
  • Process: straight-through fast-tracking for clean claims; auto-routing of risky/complex ones.
  • AI: always-on first reviewer — extract → assess → score → recommend, with a human-in-the-loop.

Future extensions: customer chatbot, automated notifications, analytics dashboards, multi-line support (home, health).

Solution Overview

Architecture / workflow

Customer ─▶ FNOL intake (Next.js form + photos)
                 │
                 ▼
        AI Pipeline (Claude API)        ┌────────────────┐
        1 Extract & validate  ─────────▶│ SQLite          │
        2 Damage & severity (vision)    │ claims + AI JSON │
        3 Fraud risk score (0–100)      └────────────────┘
        4 Decision recommendation              │
                 │                             ▼
                 ▼                    Adjuster dashboard
        Customer status tracker      (prioritized queue)
                                              │
                                              ▼
                               Approve / Investigate / Deny

AI tools used

  • In product: Claude API (claude-opus-4-8, vision + forced tool-use).
  • In development: Claude Code / Cursor / GitHub Copilot.

Key features & capabilities

  • Guided FNOL intake with photo upload and instant AI review.
  • 4-step Claude pipeline → structured, typed JSON per claim.
  • Adjuster dashboard auto-prioritized by fraud risk & status.
  • One-click human decision; full customer status tracking.

Capability descriptions

  • Extraction — normalize text/fields, flag gaps.
  • Damage — vision over photos → severity + cost range.
  • Fraud — 0–100 score with named red flags.
  • Recommendation — disposition + settlement + rationale.

User interactions

  • Customer: submit claim, upload photos, track status & outcome.
  • Adjuster: triage queue, review AI evidence, make the call.

AI intervention points (4)

  • Intake — extraction & validation
  • Damage — severity & estimate (vision)
  • Fraud — risk scoring with red flags
  • Decision — recommendation + settlement

Decision support & automation

  • Decision support: AI gives recommendation, settlement, confidence, rationale — the adjuster decides.
  • Automation: straight-through processing for low-risk claims; auto-routing of high-fraud/complex claims; future auto-drafted comms & SIU referrals.
